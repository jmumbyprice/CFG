CROSSTOWN DOUGHNUTS

London

Crosstown Doughnuts are dotted across London, from Victoria to Spitalfields. With a wide selection of sweet treats, from doughnuts to cookies, and with great options for vegans. Crosstown rolls all their own dough by hand, with all the fillings, jams, and glazes also made by them. You'll also find delicious cookies with flavours including: peanut butter and chocolate chip, oat and apricot, and chocolate chip.

Jess

JOLENE

London

Jolene is an all-day restaurant in a quiet spot opposite the park in Newington Green. During the daytime, it's a low-key brunch spot and bakery. Come evening, the candles are lit and the blackboards are replaced with a small-plates menu: think pasta, meats, cheeses and homemade bread.

Jess

POPHAMS

London

A cosy neighbourhood spot in London Fields which specialises in sourdough bread and incredible croissants by day, and a handful of pasta dishes by night. Keep an eye out for Pophams Home, their small homewares store next door, where they sell ceramics, candles, artworks and more.

Jess

THE BAR WITH SHAPES FOR A NAME

London

A hidden gem on Kingsland Road. The name itself is not actually a name, rather a description of its label which is three Bauhaus-influenced primary-coloured shapes: a yellow triangle, a red square and a blue circle (but people call it Shapes for short). It has an almost classroom or educational feel to it and there are no bottles on show, making it difficult to know the venue is a bar if you didn't already know. Excellent cocktails in an unusual setting and decent value for money, which makes it a must visit.

Jess
